Deep Learning with Mixed Precision
Stony Brook University DNI: Data and Intelligent Computing Laboratory
Deep Learning Research Assistant (2022 - PRESENT)

Many GPUs now support reduced-precision floating-point. Given the availability of such floating-point formats and the increasingly wider accessibility of their supporting hardware, reduced-precision algorithms, which work in single or double precision but carry out a part of computation in reduced precision, are now becoming a trendy research direction pursued in machine learning, programming languages, software engineering, and general scientific computing. Reduced precision alone is known to cause accuracy loss. A straightforward rounding or truncation of single or double precision data into half precision can cause overflow, underflow, or subnormal numbers, all of which affects numerical accuracy. Therefore, the central challenge of today's reduced-precision computing is how to systematically recover the lost accuracy with minor performance overhead. Our research objective is to understand reduced-precision computing and achieve new research results from the programming language perspective by targeting a smaller, less explored, yet fundamentally important area in machine learning, Neural Network

Geomagnetic Disturbance Prediction
Republic of Korea Air Force, Department of Space Weather
Deep Learning Engineer (2020 - 2022)


GeoDIP Prediction Model

A coronal hole is an open area of the magnetic field, which appears to be relatively dark in the sun due to the same density and temperature compared to the surrounding area. The Coronal Hole emits solar winds at about twice the average rate. Solar winds travel along an open magnetic field line through the coronal hole area. These areas are very shallow because coronal holes are solar regions with lower temperatures than other surrounding areas. This thin thickness contributes to the increase in solar wind speed, as particles in the stratosphere form an opportunity to break through more easily. To identify the seven-day trend of these coronal hole regions, we detect and calculate the area of the coronal hole regions using computer vision techniques based on images of the 193-A frequency band mounted on the Solar Dynamics Observatory (SDO). Through this algorithm, we calculate the polar and middle regions of the sun, and based on this, we predict coronal hole areas in each region over the next five days using Deep Learning's Long Short-Term Memory technique. This study is about how the coronal hole area changes through a deep learning model by making time-series predictions using only coronal hole area data.

Sunspot Detection and Clustering Model

Solar flares and coronal mass emissions have a huge impact on the cosmic weather around the Earth. This phenomenon creates a flow of high-energy particles inside the solar wind and the Earth's magnetosphere, and causes radiation damage to spacecraft and astronauts. The weak X-rays of the X-grade flare increase the ionization of the upper atmosphere, which affects short-wave radio communication. We developed a model for detecting sunspots to predict and prepare ahead of time for these flares to affect Aircraft missions. Sunspot is the primary cause of flares. As the Sunspot grows larger, it is more likely to explode, so checking the area and magnetic field condition of the Sunspot in real time is critical to predict the flare. We have created a model that identifies changes in magnetic field states, clusters adjacent Sunspots to form one cluster, and tracks states in real time.

Machine Learning Research Assistant (2019 - 2020)


Energy Consumption Prediction

The miniaturization of sensor devices combined with advances in networking and communication technologies have given rise to Internet of Things (IoT) paradigm. Another contemporary technology is cloud computing which facilitates availability of computing platforms, databases and applications on-demand. In this paper, we present the development of a smart home system that uses IOT and cloud computing paradigmsto predict household energy consumption and electricity costs. The proposed system collects a variety of data – such as electricity consumption, indoor temperature, humidity,outdoor weather information and uses cloud computing infrastructure for the data storage and prediction of energy consumption using timeseries forecasting methods. Users can monitor energy consumption, costs and predicted usage in real time through the dashboard application connected to cloud platform. We developed DHT11 and Arduino ESP8266 based IoT sensor to measure indoor temperature. We also wrote several services and functions in Google Cloud Platform (GCP) to collect and store sensor data and for the prediction energy consumption data. Keywords - Internet of Things, Cloud Computing, Smart Home, Weather, Energy Consumption Prediction
